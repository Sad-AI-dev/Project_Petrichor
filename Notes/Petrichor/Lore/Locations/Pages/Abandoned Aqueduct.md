# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log is a transcription of an audio fragment generated by the [[DataScraper (v3.1.53 - beta branch)]] program.

The log tracks the amount of days that have passed since the survivor was attacked by members of the [[Beetle Family]]. 
After the survivor founds themselves in the middle of the desert with no other survivors near.
This survivor is most likely from the [[UES Contact Light]], as no mention of a dropship is made and the survivor seems to have immediately become stranded upon contact with [[Petrichor V]].

The survivor avoided the assault, but managed to escape. During their escape, they fell into a [[Tar]] waterfall and recovered in an underground cave. They had a [[Healing Drone]] with them, which started to heal their leg injury.

A day later, the survivor's leg is starting to feel better, but their vision starts to go blurry. It is established that the survivor found and repaired the [[Healing Drone]] after arriving on [[Petrichor V]].
The survivor starts to grow hungry.

On day 2 after their escape, the survivor starts to feel the urge to vomit and occasionally spits out [[Tar]]. The survivor notes that the [[Healing Drone]] has stopped providing aid and actively avoids them.
This is likely due to the survivor's transformation caused by the [[Tar]] causing the drone to no longer recognize them as human.

On the 3rd day, the [[Healing Drone]] leaves and the survivor loses their vision. Their transformation is now almost complete.

The final fragment is from the 9th day, but outside the phrase `I need`, no words can be made out of the audio. These are likely the final legible words spoken by the survivor.

As the corruption progresses, the audio fragment becomes more and more distorted through `CHIT` noises. It is reasonable to assume that as the survivor begins to resemble the members of the [[Clay Family]], they begin to make noises associated with these creatures. 

During gameplay, the noises made by the [[Clay Family]] is rather different from the described `CHIT`. A possible explanation is that the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] had significantly different forms from humans, and their [[Clay Family]] forms therefore make different noises from the survivor.

Another explanation for these noises is that they are meant to resemble technology breaking down. The `CHIT` simply resembling garbled noises as the hardware fails. This would give some insight into what happens to the survivor's equipment during their transformation.

# <span class="lore-title-inline">General Notes</span>
The title for the stage suggests that this is where the [[Tar]] originally found itself after making it to [[Petrichor V]]. It is unclear if this happened at the same time as when the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] made it to [[Petrichor V]] or if it made it there on its own at another point in time.
It is clear that some members of the [[Clay Family]] made it here along with the [[Tar]], as it is established in the [[Clay Apothecary]] log that the [[Tar]] required the help of the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] to build structures similar to those seen in [[Abandoned Aqueduct]].

A notable quirk of the [[Abandoned Aqueduct]] are the blood red banners seen around the perimeter of the stage and the consequence for crossing them. During gameplay, the player gets shot with [[Tar]] pellets when crossing the boundaries for this stage.
This suggests that these banners may serve as a border that were created as some sort of agreement. 
The consequence of crossing the border is that a [[Tar]] affiliated faction opens fire on the survivor. This agreement somewhat resembles those that may be made during a war between two nations.

Massive skeletons can be seen on the stage, but it is unclear what creatures they belong to. 
These skeletons appear to be long spines with a skull. As none of the skeletons have appendages, it could be argued that these may have belonged to sea serpents or other snake-like creatures.
This lends itself to a reading that the [[Abandoned Aqueduct]] may have been an ocean at some point.
