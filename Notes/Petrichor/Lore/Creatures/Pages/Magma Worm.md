# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log is an entry from the logbook created by [[D. Furthen & Tharson]], who were aboard the [[UES [REDACTED]|UES [REDACTED].]]

[[D. Furthen & Tharson]] encountered the [[Magma Worm]] when they attempted to inspect a `curios pronged artifact`. This may be in reference to a [[Teleporter]], and a callback to the [[Magma Worm]] log from *Risk of Rain Returns*, where [[The Survivor]] also discovers a [[Magma Worm]] after approaching a [[Teleporter]] for the first time.

[[D. Furthen & Tharson]] guess the [[Magma Worm]] to be about 50 feet long, which is much smaller compared to the [[Magma Worm]] described by [[The Survivor]].
The common name assigned to the [[Magma Worm]] is simply `Magma Worm`.

They discovered several ports along the skin of the defeated [[Magma Worm]] and deduce that it must use these to propel itself forward. They also believe that this is where the flames produced by the [[Magma Worm]] come from.

The body of the [[Magma Worm]] holds heat incredibly well, maintaining a temperature of ~176.7 degrees Celsius for over 10 hours after being killed.

The [[Magma Worm]] is blind, and it's eyes are actually a symbiotic parasite that give the [[Magma Worm]] vision, as described in the [[Burning Witness]] log.

# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
[[The Survivor]] encountered the [[Magma Worm]] as the approached a [[Teleporter]] for the first time.

[[The Survivor]] guesses the [[Magma Worm]] to be roughly 200 meters in length. It has pores along its skin which produce the flames covering its body. It uses these flames to burn through the ground and to harm its targets.
[[The Survivor]] notes how the eyes of the [[Magma Worm]] lurch around looking for prey. 
The [[Burning Witness]] item log reveals that these eyes are, in fact, symbiotic organisms that help the [[Magma Worm]] see, as they are naturally blind.

Finally, [[The Survivor]] proposes that the [[Magma Worm]] must be eating vast amounts of food, or must hibernate for extremely long periods of time deep in the earth.
