# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log discusses the origin of the [[Tar]] and the unfortunate fates of the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]].

It is unknown where the [[Tar]] originated from or how it came to be. It appears to be sentient and is capable of taking over a host in order to spread to new areas. The [[Tar]] seems to be insatiable, endlessly hungering for more.
Doing so, the planet the [[Tar]] originated from was eventually completely consumed by the [[Tar]]. Yet it still needed to expand further.

Eventually, the [[Tar]] spread to a meteor that ultimately landed on the planet [[Aphelia]], home to the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]].

The [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] live in a harsh desert, where food is scarce and survival is often all but guaranteed.
The [[Tar]] took advantage of this by striking a deal with the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]]. 
This shows that the [[Tar]] is an intelligent species capable of conversation.

The deal was for the [[Tar]] to provide the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] with power, energy and sustenance. In return, the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] would help the [[Tar]] spread across [[Aphelia]].

The [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] created great infrastructure in order to assist the [[Tar]] in spreading across their planet. Individuals that especially pleased the [[Tar]] were granted new physical forms made of [[Tar]].

Only when it was much to late, did the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] realize that their reverence of the [[Tar]] had caused their own downfall, as the land, rivers, and livestock had all been consumed by the [[Tar]]. Even a great number of the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] themselves had been taken over by the [[Tar]].

On the brink of extinction, it was [[Providence]] who visited [[Aphelia]] to save the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] to save them from the demise they had brought upon themselves through the [[Tar]].
[[Providence]] had descended down from the heavens riding one of his [[Gilded Wurms]]. He extended his arm and helped the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] move to [[Petrichor V]].
In doing so, he had given the [[Tar]] a new direction to spread to, as it too, had made its way to [[Petrichor V]].

We never see uncorrupted versions of the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] during the events of the games, suggesting that the [[Tar]] ultimately killed al of those who remained from the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] before continuing to spread over [[Petrichor V]].

The members of the [[Clay Family]] are likely corrupted forms of the [[Dunepeople of Aphelia]] or were in part created by them.
