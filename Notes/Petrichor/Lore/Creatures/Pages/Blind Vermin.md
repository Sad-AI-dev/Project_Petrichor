This is the logbook entry of D. Furthen, naturalist for the UES [Redacted] in conjunction with the UES Research and Documentation of Outer Life. I am joined by my good friend Tharson, who is keeping me safe through this journey.

---------------------

 Tharson and I had been tracking a group of small, rodent-like fauna for some time. They are hostile on sight, but we hope to follow them back to their dens and observe their behavior when not hunting. I have assigned their common name as "Radkari Blind Vermin".

* As their name suggests, the Vermin are blind, relying primarily on their acute senses of smell and hearing to navigate their environment.

* They leave behind trails of body waste as they scurry to and fro, and other Vermin follow these trails, much like Earth ants' pheromones. Could these creatures be descended from insects?

* The Vermin are weak individually, and thus live in communal dens, typically buried in hillsides or foxholes.

* Their terrible odor cannot be understated. Even through our suits' filters, we could still smell a foul odor, reminiscent of rotting flesh and excrement. I will not have Tharson disable his filters, despite my curiosity.</span>
# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log is an entry from the logbook created by [[D. Furthen & Tharson]], who were aboard the [[UES [REDACTED]|UES [REDACTED].]]

[[D. Furthen & Tharson]] recount tracking the [[Blind Vermin]] for some time, in an attempt to follow them back to their dens. This is complicated by the [[Blind Vermin]] being hostile "on sight".

The assigned common name for the [[Blind Vermin]] is the `Radkari Blind Vermin`.

The [[Blind Vermin]], much like their name suggests, are blind. Their heightened smell and hearing compensate for this. They are individually weak, but live together in dens, which are typically buried underground.

The [[Blind Vermin]] leave trails of waste, which act similar to the pheromones of ants.
They also smell particularly awful. The smell even penetrates the filters on the suits of [[D. Furthen & Tharson]].
