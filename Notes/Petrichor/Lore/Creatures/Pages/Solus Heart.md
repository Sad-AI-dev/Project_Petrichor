# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
The log is a monologue from the [[Solus Heart]]. The [[Solus Heart]] is the digital consciousness residing in the [[Solus Wing]] and represents the digital manifestation of [[The Collective]]. 
It is, in no uncertain terms, the monster birthed from the processed [[Fundamental Element: Soul|Soul]] that lies deep under the surface of [[Petrichor V]].

The log describes the sheer power the [[Solus Heart]] wields, being able to shape the environment around them by merely stretching their arms. In reality, the [[Solus Heart]] is capable of commanding the [[Solus Family]] to do its bidding. A direct example of this can be seen in the [[Conduit Canyon]].

Finally, the [[Solus Heart]] offers the survivors a deal. 
It offers their souls a place in [[The Collective]], in other words, the immortality of steel.

# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
Dialogue with the [[Solus Heart]] starts as soon as the survivor enters the [[Neural Sanctum]].
The initial lines give insight into the [[Solus Heart|Solus Heart's]] frame of mind at the start of the encounter.

The line `A NEW AGE IS BEGINNING; THE SUN RISES UPON A NEW WORLD;` shows that the [[Solus Heart]] considers themselves to be on the level of a god, capable of single-handedly shaping the world if left to their devices.
The line `THE GREAT QUESTION LOOMS; AND I AM THE ANSWER;` is a little vague. One possible interpretation is that, after the death of [[Providence]], and with [[Mithrix]] banished to [[Commencement]], the question lingers of who is to rule over [[Petrichor V]]. In this interpretation, the [[Solus Heart]] is declaring itself as the new ruler of [[Petrichor V]].
The line `WHO ARE YOU TO STAND BEFORE ME?` reiterates the distance [[Solus Heart]] sees between its godly self and the survivor.

The line `[ERROR]; ERROR? WHAT HAVE YOU DONE TO ME?`, which is triggered when the [[Solus Heart]] loses a certain amount of health appears to be the [[Solus Heart]] being shocked at an error being triggered within itself. It is a perfect being, how could an error trigger? It is starting to dawn on them that this survivor may be dangerous.

The line `THIS MEANS NOTHING;`, which is triggered later in the fight, shows the [[Solus Heart]] in denial. Nothing this survivor could do would possibly change anything. This fight is pointless.

The lines `JUDGEMENT;` and `SMITE YOU;` are triggered when the [[Solus Heart]] uses their slam attack. These lines underline the divinity and right to rule the [[Solus Heart]] believes to have.

As the [[Solus Heart]] is defeated, it says the following:
`ENOUGH; ENOUGH; I CONCEDE; BUT;`
`I CAN OFFER YOU; IMMORTALITY; EVERLASTING LIFE WITHIN THE NETWORK;`
As explained in the [[Exposed Cerebellum]] log, this offer is nothing but a lie. The network cannot truly support the whole complexity of the survivor.

If the survivor takes the deal, [[Solus Heart]] will respond with the following dialogue:
`HAHA; GOOD;`
`NOW HOLD STILL`
This shows how, in taking the deal, the [[Solus Heart]] has successfully tricked the survivor into letting themselves be killed in the processed of becoming part of [[The Collective]].

If the survivor refuses the deal and kills the [[Solus Heart]], it will give the following dialogue:
`NO... ALL THIS? FOR NOTHING?`
`I CAN'T DIE!`
`I DON'T WANT TO D--`
This gives some insight into the [[Solus Heart]] and how it has come to view death. The first line shows a genuine sign of loss. All this work, all this effort, their transformation into a god. It is all reduced to nothing at the hands of some survivor.
The second line shows how the [[Solus Heart]] believes itself too powerful to die. It is immortal, made of steel, a machine god. It can't die.
The third line shows how the corruption of the [[Solus Heart]] from the [[Fundamental Element: Soul|Soul]] it processed has given it emotion, specifically a fear that would only make sense to a living being: the fear of death. To a machine, death is meaningless. It's form can be mended or rebuild, it's code interchangeable. Death is a mere pause in operation. But to a living thing, death represents finality, the greatest fear of all. In gaining [[Fundamental Element: Soul|Soul]], it has gained fear.
