# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log is a recounter by [[The Survivor]] of their battle with [[Providence]], which leads to [[Providence|Providence's]] death.

As shone during the intro cinematic of `Risk of Rain Returns`, the [[UES Contact Light]] had a [[Teleporter]] in it's cargo hold. This is possibly how [[Providence]] made his way into and / or off the vessel. This may also be [[Providence|Providence's]] reason for destroying the [[UES Contact Light]].

[[The Survivor]] recognizes [[Providence]] from the security footage of the [[UES Contact Light]], where he can be seen destroying the vessel. This likely lead to the death of almost all who were aboard the [[UES Contact Light]].

[[Providence]] is a divine figure on [[Petrichor V]], with many denizens of the planet seen revering [[Providence]] as their savior. 2 [[Gilded Wurms]] are typically found by [[Providence|Providence's]] side, acting as his left and right hands.
[[Providence]] is [[Mithrix|Mithrix's]] brother, and they are implied to share an important connection with [[She|Her]].

# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
The dialogue of the 3rd phase mostly act to illustrate how surprised [[Providence]] is by meeting considerable resistance. This goes to show that [[Providence]] was a rather peaceful ruler, rarely needing to exert force over the denizens.

The 4th phase dialogue notes the 'twist' of the fight. [[Providence]] hasn't been defeated just yet.

As [[Providence]] dies, he can say one of the following lines:
`What.. are you..?` [[Providence]], a god powerful enough to shape life was killed by something he does not understand. Perhaps [[Providence]] was aware of the human species, but he may be questioning what [[The Survivor]] really is.
Surely a species as weak as humans would never be able to kill him.

`You.. monster...` This reveals how [[Providence]] views the events of the [[UES Contact Light]].
An unknown vessel flew close to [[Petrichor V]], containing a [[Teleporter]] in its cargo hold. After destroying the ship of these invaders, a few managed to survive.
These aliens proceeded to mercilessly slaughter all those they encountered, ending their bloodbath by slaying the peaceful ruler of the planet.

He frames [[The Survivor]] as the monster. Considering the events from this perspective, perhaps this is not an incorrect interpretation. These 'monster' may truly be more afraid of [[The Survivor]] than they are of the 'monsters'.
