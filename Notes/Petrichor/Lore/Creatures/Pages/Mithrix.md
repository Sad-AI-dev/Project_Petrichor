# <span class="lore-title-inline">Lore</span>
<span class="lore">They knew to stay away from the wells - but they were just kids. And they just wanted to know.

They threw in dirt and stone and kifruit and starseed, watching as they collapsed. Thorp! The gravity wells would swallow up anything. Glass and mud and silver. Wind. Heat. Pulsar radiation.

And when his brother wasn’t watching, Mithrix would throw in worms. Thorp! But only when he wasn’t watching. You see, his brother loved worms. It would make him sad. But Mithrix didn’t care much about worms. He was just curious.

But one time, his brother was watching. And his brother loved worms.</span>
# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
This log tells the story of the young bothers [[Providence]] and [[Mithrix]], how they differed and how this would eventually lead them to clashing with each other.

The log starts by establishing the curiosity within both [[Providence]] and [[Mithrix]]. [[She]] has told them to stay away from the gravity wells, but they wanted to know.
They experimented with various substances, some visible some intangible. Notably, [[Kifruit]] and [[Starseed]] are mentioned in this list. This suggests that these species of fruit were given to them by [[She|Her]], or are native to [[Petrichor V]].

It is established here what the fundamental difference between [[Providence]] and [[Mithrix]] is. [[Providence]] has a great internal sense of compassion, while [[Mithrix]] has ambition.
[[Providence]] always loved worms. It is unclear if this refers to small worms or members of the [[Worm Family]], but this love can be seen in [[Providence]] making the [[Gilded Wurms]] his right and left hands.

However, [[Mithrix]] in his ambition, did not care for the worms, and was willing to throw worms into the gravity well just to find out what would happen. Notably, [[Mithrix]] is not without empathy, he cares for [[Providence]] and would thus only do it when his brother wasn't looking.

But one day, [[Providence]] was looking, and [[Providence]] loved worms. This plants the seeds for his eventual betrayal in [[A Moment, Fractured]].

# <span class="lore-title-inline">Notes</span>
The dialogue from [[Mithrix]] at the start of his fight with the survivors in the arena on [[Commencement]] shows that he is openly hostile to the survivors, but doesn't put great importance on their appearance. They are an obstacle that are getting in his way, nothing more.

[[Mithrix]] has more direct dialogue for the [[Heretic]], as he knows who she is. 
The line `Die again, Heretic.` specifically references the fact that the [[Heretic]] was killed by [[Providence]] while trying to take over the [[UES Contact Light]] before it was taken down.
This also shows that [[Heretic]] not only betrayed [[Providence]] by trying to escape the planet, but also angered [[Mithrix]] in the process.

[[Mithrix]] also has more direct dialogue for [[False Son]]. The line `Share in your creator's fate, false idol` shows that [[Mithrix]] believes that [[False Son]] is a botched attempt at recreating his brother, someone who could never be replaced.
The part `Share in your creator's fate` is a taunt based on the death of [[False Son|False Son's]] creator. It's not exactly clear who specifically [[Mithrix]] is referring to, as a lot of individuals took part in the creation of [[False Son]].

The lines that [[Mithrix]] says when they hurt a player in the first 3 phases are mostly generic taunts.
Some lines of note are "`Mistake`", "`Scream, Vermin`" and "`Slow`".
`Mistake` shows a small detail about what [[Mithrix]] considers to be most hurtful. [[Mithrix]] creates, and finds no greater shame than in his mistakes, such as [[Aurelionite]].
`Scream, Vermin` shows [[Mithrix|Mithrix']] habit of calling those he finds inferior "Vermin".
`Slow` is interesting, as [[Mithrix]] establishes in the [[Lunar Chimera (Wisp)]] and [[Lunar Chimera (Golem)]] logs, [[Mithrix]] believes speed to be a crucial in war.

The line `WEAK, WITHOUT YOUR BAUBLES AND TRINKETS.`, which [[Mithrix]] says during the final phase of the battle, is a reference to [[Mithrix]] taking your items during this phase.

When [[Mithrix]] kills a drone, he has a chance of saying `Inferior construct. Broken.`. This shows how [[Mithrix]] compares the drones with his various creations and how he believes the drones to be inferior.

The line [[Mithrix]] says when he kills the [[Heretic]]: `Return to pieces, Heretic.` is a reference to the fact that the [[Heretic]] was shredded to pieces and spread across the moon. These pieces can be found during gameplay as the [[Visions of Heresy]], [[Hooks of Heresy]], [[Strides of Heresy]] and [[Essence of Heresy]] item set.

The line `THE GUARDIAN...? IMPOSSIBLE...!` which is said by [[Mithrix]] when the survivor summons [[Aurelionite]] using the [[Halcyon Seed]] during the final phase of the battle is interesting because it shows how [[Mithrix]] believes it to be impossible for [[Aurelionite]] to be here. To him, [[Aurelionite]] should still be in the [[Gilded Coast]], which serves as a prison for [[Aurelionite]] which he created himself. And his creations are flawless, [[Aurelionite]] could never have escaped from it.

If [[Aurelionite]] shows up and the player is the [[Heretic]], [[Mithrix]] will comment `THE HERETIC? THE GUARDIAN? HOW? WHO PULLS YOUR STRINGS, VERMIN?`
This reaction likely stems from the fact that, to [[Mithrix]], the [[Heretic]] and [[Aurelionite]] and completely separate forces that shouldn't have any reason to work together. Therefore, there must be someone much more powerful behind this.

If both [[False Son]] and [[Aurelionite]] show up, [[Mithrix]] will exclaim `OF COURSE, THE GUARDIAN AND HER PET`. Here he comments on the relation between [[False Son]] and [[Aurelionite]], mocking [[False Son]] as nothing more than [[Aurelionite|Aurelionite's]] pet.

When [[Mithrix]] dies, he has a variety of lines he may say.
The lines `NO... NOT NOW...` and `WHY... WHY NOW...?` point to the same idea. [[Mithrix]] was on the cusp of something, about to enact some great plan. Seeing his life ended right before he could realize his ambitions is what brings him great grief in this moment.

The line `BROTHER... HELP ME...!` requires some context. [[Mithrix]] likely knows that [[Providence]] is dead, or at least that he never returned from his fight with the survivors from the [[UES Contact Light]], but here, in his final moments, he calls out to the one he loved most.
Even though that very brother betrayed him, he still loves him.

The line `THIS PLANE GROWS DARK... BROTHER... I CANNOT SEE YOU... WHERE ARE YOU...?`
This could be interpreted as what [[Mithrix]] sees as he passes on to the afterlife. In this moment, he whishes for nothing more than to be able to see his brother [[Providence]], but he cannot see him. Perhaps they are moving on to different places, perhaps it is merely a vision.

The line `BROTHER... PERHAPS... WE WILL GET IT RIGHT... NEXT TIME...` shows that [[Mithrix]], deeply and truly, regrets what happened between him and [[Providence]]. How he wishes to go back to a time where they would chase glass frogs in the sun.
